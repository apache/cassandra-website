Watch the Cassandra World Party

August 4, 2022 | Cassandra Community

« Back to the Apache Cassandra Blog

Thank you to everyone who made the second annual Cassandra World Party a great success!

The one-day virtual, free-to-attend event united the global community, and we were once again able to celebrate the people and the technology behind Apache Cassandra—while also having a lot of fun!

We are particularly grateful to all our speakers and moderators (Ben Bromhead, Chris Bradford, Ekaterina Dimitrova, Jeremy Hanna, Patrick Mcfadin and Erick Ramirez), who steered the ship well, especially during the odd technical glitch.

Catchup on the Cassandra Talks

Don’t worry if you missed out, you can now watch all the 1-hour livestream recordings for each timezone session on the Planet Cassandra YouTube channel. Session 1*: July 20 1200 UTC Session 2: July 20 1800 UTC Session 3: July 21 0100 UTC

The party was streamed live across three timezones, with 5-five-minute lightning talks, an interactive quiz, and plenty of community chatter.

Alternatively, you can dive straight into the individual five-minute talks:

Session 1:

Getting To Know (Apache) Cassandra - Sharan Foga

Data Visualisation With Apache Cassandra And Grafana - Aleks Volochnev

Cleaning Large-Scale Data In Cassandra - Rahul Xavier Singh

Tightening Security For Apache Cassandra - Maulin Vasavada

Where’s This Journey Taking Us, What’s Next For Cassandra? - Mick Semb Wever

Session 2:

Safe Os/System Upgrade By Rebooting - Serban Teodorescu

Cassandra-Backed Streaming Graph With Quine - Ryan Wright

Improved Snapshots In Cassandra 4.1 - Paulo Motta

What Is Netflix Waiting For In Apache Cassandra 4.X? - Vinay Chella

Session 3:

Using SSTable Immutability For Incremental Backup - Manish Khandelwal

Monitoring Cassandra - Michiel Saelen

Using Authorizer With CassandraDB - Lakhan Samani

Practice Of Cassandra In Instant Messaging Application - GuoWen Hong

Cassandra In Online Education (Chinese) - Li Li

Thank you, sponsors!

The 2022 Cassandra World Party was possible due to the commitment and support of our official sponsors. A huge thank you goes to AxonOps and DataStax!
